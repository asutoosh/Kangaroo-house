Moving to Delhi for college or work? One of the biggest decisions you'll face is choosing between a Paying Guest (PG) accommodation and a hostel. Both have their pros and cons, and the right choice depends on your priorities â€” budget, comfort, privacy, and lifestyle.

What is a PG?

A Paying Guest accommodation is a privately-owned property where you rent a room (single, double, or shared). PGs typically include meals, housekeeping, and utilities in the rent. They feel more like a home and offer greater flexibility compared to institutional hostels.

What is a Hostel?

Hostels are usually attached to educational institutions. They have strict rules, fixed meal times, and limited room choices. While hostels are often cheaper, they come with curfews, regulations on visitors, and less personal space.

Key Differences: PG vs Hostel

  • Privacy: PGs offer single/double rooms; hostels typically have 4-6 person dorms
  • Food Quality: PGs serve homely meals with variety; hostel mess food can be repetitive
  • Flexibility: PGs have relaxed rules; hostels enforce strict curfews and timings
  • Location: PGs can be closer to specific colleges; hostel locations are fixed
  • Safety: Good PGs offer CCTV, biometric entry; hostels have basic security
  • Maintenance: PGs include housekeeping; hostel cleaning may be inconsistent
  • Community: Both offer social environments, but PGs tend to have smaller, closer groups

When to Choose a Hostel

Choose a hostel if you're on a very tight budget, want to be directly on campus, or prefer the regimented structure that hostels provide. Hostels can work well for students who don't mind shared dorms and fixed schedules.
