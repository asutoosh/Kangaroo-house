Shared Accommodation

Definition: Shared accommodation means living with one or more people in the same room, sharing the room space and sometimes common facilities like bathrooms and kitchens.

Detailed Explanation

Shared accommodation is the most affordable option in PGs and hostels. It typically comes in twin sharing (2 persons per room) or triple sharing (3 persons per room) configurations. Each resident gets their own bed, storage space, and often a study desk. Common facilities like bathrooms may be shared or attached depending on the PG. Shared rooms are especially popular among students who benefit from the social aspect and lower costs.

Key Points to Know

  • Twin sharing = 2 persons, Triple sharing = 3 persons
  • Each person gets their own bed and storage
  • 30-50% cheaper than single occupancy rooms
  • Great for making friends and study partners
  • Some PGs allow you to choose your roommate
