Paying Guest (PG)

Definition: A Paying Guest (PG) is a renter who lives in someone else's home or a managed residential property, typically with meals and basic services included in the rent.

Detailed Explanation

The PG accommodation model originated in India as a way for homeowners to rent out spare rooms to students and professionals. Unlike traditional renting, PG arrangements typically include furnished rooms, daily meals, housekeeping, and utilities in a single monthly payment. Today, professional PG operators like Kangaroo House manage dedicated properties specifically designed for PG living, offering amenities like WiFi, laundry, and 24/7 security.

Key Points to Know

  • PG rent usually includes meals, WiFi, and housekeeping
  • Most PGs offer single, twin, or triple sharing options
  • PG agreements are typically month-to-month
  • Security deposit is usually 1-2 months' rent
  • Many PGs are gender-specific (girls-only or boys-only)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between PG and hostel?

A PG typically offers a more home-like, smaller-scale experience with meals and services, while hostels are larger dormitory-style facilities. PGs usually have better food quality and more personalised management.

Is PG good for students?

Yes, PGs are ideal for students because they provide furnished rooms, home-cooked meals, and a safe environment, allowing students to focus entirely on their studies without household responsibilities.
