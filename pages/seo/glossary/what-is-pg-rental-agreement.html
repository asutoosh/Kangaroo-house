PG Agreement / Rental Agreement

Definition: A PG agreement is a written contract between the PG operator and resident outlining rent, deposit, rules, notice period, and other terms of stay.

Detailed Explanation

A PG agreement protects both the resident and the operator. It documents the monthly rent, security deposit amount, lock-in period, notice period, house rules, meal inclusions, and maintenance responsibilities. While not all PGs provide formal agreements, it's strongly recommended to have one. The agreement should be signed by both parties and each should keep a copy. In case of disputes, this document serves as legal proof of the agreed terms.

Key Points to Know

  • Documents rent, deposit, and stay terms
  • Should be signed by both parties
  • Includes house rules and meal details
  • Serves as legal proof in disputes
  • Keep your copy safe throughout your stay

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a PG agreement legally binding?

Yes, a signed PG agreement is a legally binding document. It protects both the resident's and operator's rights and can be used as evidence in case of disputes.

What should a PG agreement include?

A comprehensive PG agreement should include: monthly rent, security deposit amount, lock-in period, notice period, meal details, WiFi and utility inclusions, house rules, and termination conditions.

Can I stay without a PG agreement?

While it's possible, it's not recommended. Without an agreement, you have no legal protection regarding your deposit refund, rent hikes, or dispute resolution.
